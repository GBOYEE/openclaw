# CivicSentinel — AI-Powered Civic Integrity Monitor

**Problem:** Elections and public discourse are threatened by deepfakes, coordinated misinformation campaigns, and rumor propagation on social media. Journalists and watchdogs lack tools to detect and track these at scale.

**Solution:** CivicSentinel — an open-source platform that:
- Monitors Twitter/X, Facebook, Telegram channels for viral suspicious content
- Uses computer vision (LLaVA) to detect deepfake images/videos
- Analyzes text for bot‑like patterns (coordination, sentiment spikes)
- Maps spread networks (retweet graphs) to identify amplifiers
- Alerts journalists and fact‑checkers in real time

**Target grants:** Mozilla AI & Democracy Awards, Google AI for Government Innovation, Ford Foundation (democracy), Fast Grants (disinformation), US DOD (if partnered with university).

**Success (18 months):**
- Monitor 10k+ accounts in target regions (Nigeria, Kenya, US swing states)
- Detect 100+ deepfakes or viral misinformation events
- Provide early warnings to partner fact‑checkers (e.g., Africa Check)
- Publish open-source tooling adopted by 50+ newsrooms

**Tech:** Python, agent‑core for planning detection tasks, LLaVA for vision, NLTK/spaCy for text, NetworkX for graph analysis, FastAPI dashboard.

**Why fundable:** Protects democracy; timely; open source builds trust; aligns with global press freedom initiatives.
